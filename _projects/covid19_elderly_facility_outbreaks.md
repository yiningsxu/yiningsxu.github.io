## COVID-19 Outbreak Size in Elderly Residential Facilities

**Project:** Determinants of COVID-19 outbreak size in elderly residential facilities in Okinawa Prefecture, Japan

- **Study period:** April-June 2022
- **Role:** First author; data preprocessing, statistical analysis, figure/table preparation, and manuscript writing
- **Design:** Questionnaire-based cross-sectional study of facilities with reported COVID-19 outbreaks

This project examined why some COVID-19 outbreaks in elderly residential facilities remained small while others expanded. Using data from 127 outbreaks across 78 facilities in Okinawa Prefecture, the study linked outbreak size with facility characteristics, infection-control practices, case-detection routes, and operational constraints.

Negative binomial regression was used to estimate facility-level factors associated with outbreak size. Contact-based staff testing was associated with substantially smaller outbreaks than routine staff RT-PCR screening (adjusted risk ratio 0.11; 95% CI 0.03-0.37). Resident mask wearing was also associated with smaller outbreak size (adjusted risk ratio 0.40; 95% CI 0.16-0.99).

The findings suggest that exposure-triggered testing and practical resident protection may be more effective than fixed-interval screening alone when diagnostic resources are limited. The study also highlights the need to support care staff and facility operations during outbreaks, rather than treating infection control as a purely individual-level behavior.
