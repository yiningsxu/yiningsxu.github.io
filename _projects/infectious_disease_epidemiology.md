## Infectious Disease Epidemiology in Okinawa

**Project:** Evidence evaluation for COVID-19 screening, outbreak prevention, and setting-specific transmission in Okinawa Prefecture

- **Study period:** 2020-2022 surveillance and screening data; publications in 2024-2026
- **Institutions:** Kyoto University; Okinawa Prefecture epidemiological and statistical analysis collaborators; domestic and international research partners
- **Role:** First author and lead analyst for the elderly residential facility outbreak-size study; second author and collaborator for the airport fever screening and school/childcare transmission studies

This research line uses administrative surveillance, screening-program, and facility questionnaire data from Okinawa Prefecture to quantify where public-health screening and infection-control strategies worked, where they were limited, and how intervention priorities should shift under constrained diagnostic capacity.

### Focus Areas

**Elderly residential facilities.** A questionnaire-based cross-sectional study of 127 COVID-19 outbreaks across 78 elderly residential facilities in April-June 2022 used negative binomial regression to identify facility-level determinants of outbreak size. Contact-based staff testing was associated with markedly smaller outbreaks than routine staff RT-PCR screening (aRR 0.11; 95% CI 0.03-0.37), and resident mask-wearing was also associated with smaller outbreak size (aRR 0.40; 95% CI 0.16-0.99). The findings emphasize risk-based, exposure-triggered testing together with operational support for care staff.

**Airport fever screening.** A retrospective evaluation of thermographic screening across nine airports from May 2020 to March 2022 covered 9,003,616 arriving passengers and 5,712,983 departing passengers at Naha Airport. Fever-only screening had a low estimated capture rate for infected domestic travelers, up to 8.2% (95% CI 4.00-14.56%), indicating that temperature screening alone had limited value as a border-control measure.

**School and childcare settings.** An analysis of 897 Okinawa School PCR Project events during January-March 2022 compared secondary SARS-CoV-2 transmission by index-case role and setting. Most events detected no secondary cases (73.1%). Teacher/staff-index events in nurseries and kindergartens detected secondary cases in 32.3% of events (`R_event` 0.62; 95% CI 0.53-0.72), whereas teacher/staff-index events in elementary and secondary schools did so in 12.8% (`R_event` 0.15; 95% CI 0.06-0.33). The results suggest prioritizing childcare-worker screening and rapid contact testing when testing capacity is limited.
