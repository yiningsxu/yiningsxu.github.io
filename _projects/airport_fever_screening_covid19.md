## Airport Fever Screening for SARS-CoV-2

**Project:** Assessment of fever screening at airports in detecting domestic passengers infected with SARS-CoV-2 in Okinawa Prefecture

- **Study period:** May 2020-March 2022
- **Role:** Second author; data visualization and collaborative analysis
- **Design:** Retrospective evaluation of thermographic fever screening across Okinawa airports

This project assessed whether temperature-based airport screening could meaningfully detect domestic passengers infected with SARS-CoV-2. The study analyzed fever-screening records from nine airports in Okinawa Prefecture, including 9,003,616 arriving passengers and 5,712,983 departing passengers at Naha Airport.

The analysis compared the number of passengers detected by thermographic screening with estimated infected travelers based on prefecture-level epidemic conditions. Fever screening captured only a small fraction of infected domestic passengers; the estimated capture rate reached at most 8.2% (95% CI 4.00-14.56%).

These findings indicate that fever-only screening has limited effectiveness as a stand-alone border-control measure, especially when many infected travelers are asymptomatic, pre-symptomatic, or afebrile. The project supports a more layered approach to travel-related risk management, combining temperature checks with context-sensitive measures such as testing, risk communication, and surveillance.
