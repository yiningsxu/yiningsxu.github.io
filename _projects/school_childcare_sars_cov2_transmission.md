## SARS-CoV-2 Transmission in Schools and Childcare Settings

**Project:** Secondary SARS-CoV-2 transmission from childcare workers versus teachers in Okinawa school-associated screening events

- **Study period:** January-March 2022
- **Role:** Second author; collaborative analysis and manuscript contribution
- **Design:** Event-level analysis of school-associated PCR screening data

This project evaluated how secondary SARS-CoV-2 transmission differed by educational setting and by whether the index case was a child/student or a teacher/staff member. The study used 897 events from the Okinawa School PCR Project during a period of intense Omicron transmission.

Most screening events detected no secondary cases (73.1%), but transmission patterns differed sharply across settings. In nurseries and kindergartens, teacher/staff-index events detected secondary cases in 32.3% of events, with an event reproduction number (`R_event`) of 0.62 (95% CI 0.53-0.72). In elementary and secondary schools, teacher/staff-index events detected secondary cases in 12.8% of events, with `R_event` of 0.15 (95% CI 0.06-0.33).

The findings suggest that childcare workers may play a larger role in secondary transmission than teachers in older school settings, likely reflecting closer physical contact and care routines in nurseries and kindergartens. When testing capacity is limited, the study supports prioritizing childcare-worker screening and rapid contact testing in early-childhood settings.
